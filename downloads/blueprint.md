# 🧭 Unemployed & Surviving: 90-Day Money Control Kit
### A Notion Template Guide — Build This Step by Step

> **A note before you begin:** Losing a job is hard. This template isn't here to judge your spending or pressure you — it's here to give you clarity. Clarity is calming. You've got this.

---

## 🗂️ HOW TO SET UP THE WORKSPACE

1. Create a new **full-page Notion page** titled:
   `🧭 Unemployed & Surviving — 90-Day Money Control Kit`
2. Set the page icon to 🧭 and add a **cover image** (Notion's built-in "Gradients" collection → pick something soft and calming — a muted blue or warm amber works well)
3. Add this callout block at the very top:

**Callout block:**
> 💛 *You are not behind. You are not failing. You are navigating something hard — and having a plan means you're already ahead of where you were yesterday. Take it one week at a time.*

4. Below the callout, add a **Divider** block, then a **Table of Contents** block so every section is jump-linked.

---

#### 1D — Daily Spending Limit Formula

**Add an H3:** `📊 Your Daily Spending Limit`

**Add a Callout block (green/🟢):**
> **Formula:**
> (Total Income) − (Fixed Bills) − (Variable Budget) = **Money Left Over**
> Money Left Over ÷ 30 = **Daily Limit**
>
> 💡 If this number is zero or negative, flag your highest variable expenses for cuts — not all at once, one at a time.

**Text blocks (fill these in):**
```
Total Income:         $______
− Fixed Bills:        $______
− Variable Budget:    $______
= Money Left Over:    $______

÷ 30 days =

🎯 My Daily Spending Limit: $______
```

> 💡 *Notion tip: Use a `Code` block (monospace) for the formula so it stays neatly aligned — type `/code` to insert it.*

## SECTION 2 — 🚫 No-Spend Challenge Tracker

> **Purpose:** Build momentum and awareness. No-spend days aren't punishment — they're proof that you're in control.

---

**Add an H2:** `🚫 No-Spend Challenge Tracker`

**Add a Callout block (blue/💙):**
> A "no-spend day" = you spent $0 on anything non-essential. Fixed bills that auto-pay don't count. Even getting 10 of these in a month is a win.

---

### Block Structure

**Add an H3:** `📅 30-Day Grid`

**Create a Simple Table (6 columns × 5 rows + header):**

| Week | Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Day 1 | Day 2 | Day 3 | Day 4 | Day 5 | Day 6 | Day 7 |
| Week 2 | Day 8 | Day 9 | Day 10 | Day 11 | Day 12 | Day 13 | Day 14 |
| Week 3 | Day 15 | Day 16 | Day 17 | Day 18 | Day 19 | Day 20 | Day 21 |
| Week 4 | Day 22 | Day 23 | Day 24 | Day 25 | Day 26 | Day 27 | Day 28 |
| Wrap-Up | Day 29 | Day 30 | — | — | — | — | — |

**How to use:**
- ✅ = No-spend day
- ❌ = Spending day (no shame — just mark it)
- 🌟 = No-spend AND applied to a job today

> 💡 *Notion tip: Click each cell and type the emoji directly. You can also use Notion's `/emoji` shortcut. Resize the table columns to be equal-width for a clean grid look.*

---

**Add an H3 below the grid:** `🏆 Monthly Totals`

**Text blocks:**
```
✅ No-spend days this month:   _____ / 30
🌟 Power days (no-spend + job applied): _____
💬 How did it feel overall? ______________________
🎁 My reward for hitting my goal: ______________________
```

## SECTION 4 — 📋 Job Application Tracker

---

**Add an H2:** `📋 Job Application Tracker`

**Add a Callout block (purple/💜):**
> Aim for quality over quantity. 5 thoughtful applications beat 50 copy-paste ones. Track everything — your future self will thank you.

---

### Block Structure

**Create an Inline Database** (type `/database` → "Table — Inline")

**Name it:** `Applications`

**Properties:**

| Property Name | Type | Options / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Company | **Title** | |
| Role / Job Title | **Text** | |
| Date Applied | **Date** | |
| Application Source | **Select** | LinkedIn, Indeed, Company Site, Referral, Recruiter, Cold Outreach, Other |
| Status | **Select** | 📤 Applied, 👀 Under Review, 📞 Phone Screen, 🎤 Interview, 📝 Take-Home Test, 🤝 Final Round, ✅ Offer, ❌ Rejected, 🔇 Ghosted, ⏸️ On Hold |

```

Interviews:            _____
```

## SECTION 5 — 📆 Weekly Money Check-In

> **Purpose:** A 5-minute weekly ritual to stay grounded. Not a guilt session — a gut check.

---

**Add an H2:** `📆 Weekly Money Check-In`

**Add a Callout block (green/💚):**
> Do this every Sunday (or whatever day feels right). Pour a coffee, get comfortable, and just answer honestly. There are no wrong answers here.

---

### Block Structure

**Create an Inline Database** (type `/database` → "Table — Inline")

**Name it:** `Weekly Check-Ins`

**Properties:**

| Property Name | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Week Of | **Title** | Format: "Week of April 14" |
| Did I stay within my daily limit? | **Select** | ✅ Yes, Most Days / ⚠️ Mostly, A Few Slips / ❌ No — Need to Reset |
| Biggest spending win | **Text** | Something you're proud of |
| Hardest moment this week | **Text** | What tempted you or stressed you out? |
| Something I can cut next week | **Text** | One specific thing |
| How I'm feeling overall | **Select** | 😌 Calm & Steady / 😤 Frustrated / 😟 Anxious / 😴 Burnt Out / 💪 Determined / 😊 Hopeful |
| One thing I did for job hunting | **Text** | Even one application counts |
| Wins to celebrate | **Text** | No win is too small — write it down |

> 💡 *Notion tip: Open each week's row as a full page for more writing room. You can also add a **Gallery view** of this database filtered by month to see your emotional journey over time — it's surprisingly motivating.*

---

**Add this Text block below the database:**
> 🌱 *Reminder: You don't have to have a perfect week to have a good check-in. Honesty > perfection, every time.*

## SECTION 6 — 🚨 Emergency Expense Log

> **Purpose:** Track the unexpected so it stops feeling random. Patterns emerge — and patterns can be planned for.

---

**Add an H2:** `🚨 Emergency Expense Log`

**Add a Callout block (red/❤️):**
> An "emergency" is anything unplanned that forced you to spend money. Log it without shame. The goal is awareness, not punishment.

---

### Block Structure

**Create an Inline Database** (type `/database` → "Table — Inline")

**Name it:** `Emergency Expenses`

**Properties:**

| Property Name | Type | Options / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Description | **Title** | What was it? |
| Date | **Date** | |
| Amount | **Number** | Format: Dollar |
| Category | **Select** | Car Repair, Medical, Home/Appliance, Pet, Family Obligation, Tech/Device, Overdraft Fee, Other |
| Was it avoidable? | **Select** | ✅ No — True Emergency / ⚠️ Maybe — Could've Planned Better / ❌ Yes — Impulse or Poor Timing |
| How did I cover it? | **Select** | Savings, Credit Card, Borrowed, Side Hustle Income, Delayed Another Bill, Other |
| What would prevent this next time? | **Text** | One-line answer is fine |
| Stress level at time | **Select** | 😐 Minor / 😬 Stressful / 😰 Very Stressful / 🆘 Crisis |

---

**Add a Text block below:**
```
📊 Emergency Expense Summary (update monthly):
Total emergency spending this month: $______
Most common category: ______
Lessons learned: ______________________________
```

**Add a Callout block (yellow/💛):**
> 💡 **Micro-emergency fund goal:** Even saving $5–10/week builds a small buffer. Try to set aside just one "oops fund" amount per week, even if it's tiny. $200 in a savings account prevents a lot of credit card debt.
